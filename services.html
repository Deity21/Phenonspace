Is Outsourcing Video Game Development Services a Good Idea?

Stepping into the unknown is a leap of faith, yet you can be generously rewarded afterward. Trying outsourcing for the first time might feel frightening, but a trusted partner will help you safely tread this uncharted territory.

When you hire a good game development studio, outsourcing brings a number of fruitful benefits:

  • Fast Time-to-Market. Delegating development and/or art production allows for tandem work with the vendor. Hence, you speed up the game development process, reducing the time to launch and boosting your ROI.
  • Access to Expertise. Outsourcing lets you tap into specialized expertise you may not have in-house. Thus, you use the necessary resources without the need to hire any specialists on a permanent basis.
  • Cost-effectiveness. Outsourcing is a cost-effective option when you look for 2D and 3D game development services nearshore/offshore. You can choose a region with lower labor costs than yours but with the same quality of provided gaming solutions, e.g., Ukraine or Poland.
  • Flexibility.  When working with a gamedev partner, you can increase and decrease the size of the game development and art production teams as your requirements change.

However, outsourcing could become a disaster if you pick an unreliable partner. You may encounter communication and quality problems as well as burdensome delays.

How Long Does It Take To Develop a Video Game?

Games of different complexity require various development timeframes. What also influences the time it takes to develop a title is the team’s size, project scope, game length, target platforms, and funding.

Suppose you have an average size team and sufficient funding; how much time would you need?

Small and Indie Games

If you’re aiming for small and indie titles, such as puzzle games, match 3, or platformers, note

that they have simple mechanics and visuals; therefore, they don’t take much time to develop. Commonly, game developers spend from a few months to a year to create one. For example, Candy Crush , a match 3 puzzle game for mobile, had its soft launch a year after the start of development.

Mid-Sized and Genre-Specific Games

These games have more detailed visuals and more complex gameplay mechanics that make them longer to create. Mid-sized games can be narrative-driven, offer non-linear gameplay, or be a mix of the first two. As for genres, these titles can debut as role-playing games, action adventures, platformers, simulations, survival games, and so much more.

Medium-sized video game dev services usually require 1 to 3 years of development. A Way Out , an action-adventure game, took approximately 3 years to make.

AAA and Highly Complex Games

Such titles feature intricate gameplay, highly advanced graphics, expansive worlds, and immersive narratives. Like mid-sized, AAA games can be linear (narrative-driven adventures like The Last of Us or action-packed journeys like Uncharted ), non-linear (open-world epics like The Witcher 3 or sandbox adventures like Grand Theft Auto V ), or hybrid (structured open worlds like Horizon Forbidden West or player-driven experiences like Cyberpunk 2077 ).

Highly complex 3D games call for 3-5+ years to build. The creation of Horizon Forbidden West lasted for 5 years, and The Last of Us was being developed for a bit more.
